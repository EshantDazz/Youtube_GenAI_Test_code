Zero-Shot and Few-Shot Prompting

Advanced Techniques in LLM Prompting

Zero-Shot Prompting
  • Concept: Involves prompting a large language model (LLM) to perform a task without providing any specific examples or demonstrations within the prompt itself. The model relies solely on its pre-trained knowledge and the instructions crafted in the prompt.
  • Example:

    Imagine you want to classify the sentiment of a movie review. Here's a zero-shot prompt:

    Prompt: Classify the following text into positive, negative, or neutral sentiment:

The movie was a bore. The acting was terrible, and the plot was predictable.

    The LLM, understanding the concepts of sentiment and classification based on its training, should classify the review as "negative" without any prior examples of classified reviews.

  • Applications: Zero-shot prompting is useful for tasks where labeled data is scarce or creating specific examples might be difficult. It's a good starting point for sentiment analysis, text summarization, or question answering tasks.
Few-Shot Prompting
  • Concept: Similar to zero-shot prompting, but the prompt incorporates a few examples to guide the LLM towards the desired outcome. These examples showcase the task being performed correctly.
  • Example:

    Let's revisit the sentiment analysis scenario. Here's a few-shot prompt with examples:

    Prompt: Classify the following text into positive, negative, or neutral sentiment:

* Text: I loved this movie! It was hilarious and kept me engaged throughout. (Positive)
* Text: The acting was decent, but the story felt unoriginal. (Neutral)
* Text: A complete disappointment. Stay away from this movie! (Negative)

Now, classify this new text: 
The scenery was beautiful, but the plot was weak.

    By providing classified examples, the LLM can grasp the nuances of sentiment analysis better and likely classify the new text as "neutral" based on the provided examples.

  • Applications: Few-shot prompting is generally more effective than zero-shot, especially for complex tasks. It offers a balance between ease of use and improved accuracy for tasks like sentiment analysis, text classification, or machine translation.
In Essence
  • Zero-shot prompting - Instructing the LLM without specific examples (like giving directions without a map).
  • Few-shot prompting - Providing a few examples alongside instructions (like giving directions with a basic hand-drawn map).

Remember, the more examples you provide (few-shot becomes many-shot prompting), the better the LLM can understand the task and potentially improve its performance.
