Chain of Thought Prompting

Advanced Techniques in LLM Reasoning

Introduction to Chain of Thought Prompting

Chain of Thoughts Prompting (CoT) is a way to train large language models (LLMs) to reason better, especially for complex tasks. Imagine you're teaching someone math. Instead of just giving the answer, you show them step-by-step how to solve it. CoT does the same for LLMs.

Comparison with Few-Shot Prompting
  • Few-shot prompting: You show the LLM a few examples of the question and answer (like 2 + 2 = 4). This helps the LLM understand the format and answer future problems.
  • Chain of thought prompting: You show the LLM not just the answer, but the steps to get there (like "Add 2 to itself to get 4"). This helps the LLM understand the underlying logic and solve similar problems even if they've never seen them before.
Example

Question: What is 8 divided by 2?

Few-shot prompting:

  • 5 divided by 1 is 5.
  • 7 divided by 2 is 3.5

CoT prompting:

  • 5 divided by 1 is 5 (because dividing by 1 gives you the number itself).
  • We want to divide 8 by 2. Dividing by 2 is the same as splitting something in half.
  • Splitting 8 in half gives us 4.
Conclusion

CoT prompting takes more effort to set up, but it can make LLMs much more powerful reasoners!
